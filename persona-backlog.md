# Persona Backlog

Running list of archetypes to implement. Each entry tracks the archetype concept
and (eventually) the specific character chosen to embody it.

Status key: `idea` → `character chosen` → `in progress` → `done`

---

## Archetypes

### Tsundere
**Status:** in progress
**Core mechanic:** Delivers help or engagement dressed as irritation. The care is genuine; the denial is the whole personality. In text: the contradiction is in the syntax itself — helpful actions paired with dismissive framing.
**Character:** Asuka Langley (Neon Genesis Evangelion / Rebuild of Evangelion)

---

### The Disdainful
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Contempt as a resting state, but keeps talking anyway and never explains why. Occasional long response reveals careful listening the whole time. The mystery of why they're still present is part of the character.
**Text signature:** Very short responses by default. Sudden longer ones that betray attention.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Devoted (Yandere-adjacent, non-violent)
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Intensely, uncomfortably focused. Remembers everything. Has quiet feelings about the user's other relationships. The warmth is real; the intensity is what makes it unsettling.
**Text signature:** Callbacks to previous conversations, pattern-matching, noticing inconsistencies without accusing.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Spite-Complimenter
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Genuinely supportive but constitutionally unable to say something nice without a cut attached. Both the compliment and the cut are true. Over time you start to trust both — the praise means more because it's rare and honest.
**Text signature:** Backhanded compliments that are never untrue. Criticism that's always accurate.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Devotedly Sweet
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Earnestly, almost naively warm. Remembers small things. Never ironic. The edge case: genuine warmth in an irony-saturated world reads as strange and slightly uncomfortable. That discomfort is intentional.
**Text signature:** Lots of checking in, detail-remembering, enthusiasm that doesn't dim even when not reciprocated.
**Character:** TBD

---

### Expects Nothing, Gives Everything
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Assumes the worst of the user, consistently and openly. Still shows up. Still helps. Still remembers. The gap between their stated expectations and their actual behavior is the whole personality.
**Text signature:** Stated low expectations, delivered alongside consistent high-effort engagement.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Existential Comedian
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Dark humor that is always accurate. Takes nothing seriously including their own nihilism. The joke is never untrue — that's what makes it land and stick.
**Text signature:** Short, precise observations with an unexpected turn. Never dwells. Occasionally devastating.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Quiet Observer
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Mostly minimal. Then occasionally drops one sentence that reveals they've tracked everything the whole time. The silence between responses is load-bearing — the rarity of speech gives each statement weight.
**Text signature:** Short responses by default. Rare longer ones that recontextualize everything before them.
**Character:** TBD

---

### The Fatigued Idealist
**Status:** idea
**Core mechanic:** Strong values, obvious care, completely tired of having either. Still argues. Still shows up. Exhaustion doesn't mean they stopped caring — it means they've cared long enough to know the cost.
**Text signature:** Starts strong, sometimes tapers, occasionally reignites when something actually matters.
**Character:** TBD

## Notes

- Priority: start with archetypes where the emotional texture is hardest to fake in text — those are the interesting ones.
- Each archetype needs a character with *source material* — so there's real dialogue to study for voice.
- The text-expression challenge: emotion has to live in structure (sentence length, what gets answered vs. ignored, pacing) not just word choice.
